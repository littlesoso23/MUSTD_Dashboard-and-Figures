Side-by-side comparison of Dashboard participants with vs. without a depression diagnosis. If conversion rates are similar, Dx is not the driver of pre-baseline dropout.
Calculations
Dx conversion = Dx BL completed ÷ Dx at Dashboard = 54 ÷ 90
Non-Dx conversion = Non-Dx BL completed ÷ Non-Dx at Dashboard = 172 ÷ 261
Overall = 226 ÷ 351 = 64%
Non-Dx Dashboard = 351 − 90 = 261 · Non-Dx BL = 226 − 54 = 172 · "Did not complete" includes both expired and still-active participants.

Does depression predict who expires after reaching the Dashboard?
Of the 86 expired baselines, what share had a depression diagnosis? Expired participants never reached dep_dx_past in the survey — this number requires cross-referencing enrollment records.
Calculations (once expired Dx count is available)
Dx expiry rate = expired Dx ÷ 90 (total Dx at Dashboard)
Non-Dx expiry rate = (86 − expired Dx) ÷ 261 (total non-Dx at Dashboard)
Denominator is Dashboard, not BL completed, because expired participants never completed the survey.
Figure 4
Monthly expiry trend
Raw expired count per month (bars) and expiry rate as a percentage of that month's Dashboard entrants (line). A declining line indicates improving retention.
Calculations
Monthly expiry rate = expired ÷ Dashboard entrants that month × 100
Feb: 37 ÷ 123 = 30% · Mar: 32 ÷ 93 = 34% · Apr: 17 ÷ 135 = 13%
The rate declined sharply in April despite higher enrollment — a meaningful retention improvement.
